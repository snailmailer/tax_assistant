Every Canadian resident must file an annual T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). The tax year runs January 1 to December 31, and you report the prior year's income each spring.
Who Must File?
You owe federal or provincial/territorial income tax
You want to claim a refund
The CRA sent you a request to file
You disposed of capital property or had capital gains
You received a demand to file from CRA
You want to claim the Canada Workers Benefit, GST/HST credit, or other benefits

Core Forms for All Individuals
If you earn income from an employer, your taxes are deducted from each paycheque. At year-end, your employer provides a T4 slip with your income and deduction details. You still must file a T1 General return.
Step-by-Step Process — Employed Individual
Step 1: Gather Your Documents
T4 slip(s) from every employer (received by end of February)
T4A slips if you received any pension, EI, or other income
RRSP contribution receipts
Tuition receipts (T2202) if applicable
Medical expense receipts
Charitable donation receipts
Notice of Assessment from the previous year
Social Insurance Number (SIN)
Your bank account details for direct deposit of any refund
Step 2: Obtain the T1 General Form
Online: Download from canada.ca/taxes
In-person: Pick up at Canada Post or your local CRA office
Tax software: Use CRA-certified software (TurboTax, Wealthsimple Tax, StudioTax, H&R Block) which auto-populates many fields
Step 3: Complete the T1 General
See the detailed T1 field-by-field guide in Section 5 below.
Step 4: Submit Your Return
NETFILE: File online through CRA-certified software — fastest, refunds in 8 business days
EFILE: Have a tax professional file on your behalf electronically
Paper mail: Print and mail to your regional CRA tax centre
Step 5: After Filing
You will receive a Notice of Assessment (NOA) confirming CRA's review
Set up direct deposit in My CRA Account for faster refunds
Keep all records for at least 6 years
Self-employed individuals (freelancers, sole proprietors, contractors, gig workers) must report all business income and expenses. No employer withholds taxes, so you are fully responsible for calculating and remitting what you owe.
Step-by-Step Process — Self-Employed Individual
Step 1: Organize Your Records All Year
Track all business income (invoices, receipts, platform statements from Uber, Etsy, Fiverr, etc.)
Keep all business expense receipts (office supplies, travel, phone, internet, home office, professional fees)
Maintain a mileage log if using a vehicle for business
Track GST/HST you collected (if applicable)
Step 2: Gather All Documents
T4A slips from clients (if they issued them)
All income records including cash payments
All business expense receipts
Previous year's Notice of Assessment
Home office measurements (if claiming home office expenses)
Vehicle log and total kilometres driven
SIN and CRA Business Number (if registered)
Step 3: Complete Form T2125
The T2125 (Statement of Business or Professional Activities) is attached to your T1 and calculates your net business income. See the detailed T2125 field guide in Section 6.
Step 4: Transfer Net Income to T1 General
Your net business income from T2125 goes on Line 13500 of your T1
Add this to any other income (employment T4, investment income, etc.)
Your total income determines your federal and provincial tax brackets
Step 5: Calculate CPP Contributions (Schedule 8)
Self-employed pay both employee AND employer portions of CPP
For 2025: 11.9% of net self-employment income, up to a maximum of $8,068.20
The employer portion (half) is deductible on your T1
Complete Schedule 8 to calculate your CPP contribution
Step 6: GST/HST Registration and Filing
If your gross revenue exceeds $30,000 over 4 consecutive calendar quarters, you MUST register for a GST/HST number
Once registered, charge GST/HST on invoices, collect it, and remit it to CRA
File a separate GST/HST return (quarterly, annually, or monthly depending on your reporting period)
Step 7: File Your Return
Deadline to FILE: June 15, 2026 (for 2025 tax year)
Deadline to PAY any balance owing: April 30, 2026
Submit via NETFILE, EFILE through a professional, or mail paper return
Step 8: Consider Quarterly Installment Payments
If you expect to owe more than $3,000 in net tax, CRA may require quarterly installments
Installments due: March 15, June 15, September 15, and December 15
CRA sends installment reminders if they believe you need to pay

Method 1: NETFILE (Recommended — Fastest)
Create or sign into your CRA My Account at canada.ca/my-cra-account
Download CRA-certified tax software: Wealthsimple Tax (free), TurboTax, StudioTax, H&R Block, GenuTax
Enter all your information into the software — use Auto-fill My Return to import slips
Review your return and ensure everything is accurate
Click "File" — the software transmits your return directly to CRA
Save your NETFILE confirmation number — proof your return was received
Set up direct deposit in CRA My Account to receive your refund within 8 business days
Method 2: EFILE (Through a Tax Professional)
Hire a registered tax professional (accountant, tax preparer, H&R Block office)
Gather and provide all your documents to them
The professional completes your return including T2125 if self-employed
They submit electronically using the CRA's EFILE system
You receive a copy of your return and the EFILE confirmation
Method 3: Paper Filing (Slowest — 12+ weeks processing)
Download and print the T1 General package from canada.ca/taxes, or request it by phone: 1-800-959-8281
Complete all forms by hand or by typing into fillable PDFs
Attach all supporting forms (T2125, Schedule 8, etc.) and attach copies of all slips
Do NOT attach original slips — keep originals for your records
Mail to your regional CRA tax centre (address varies by province — see CRA website)
Keep a copy of everything you mail
Notice of Assessment (NOA)
CRA will send a Notice of Assessment after processing your return — typically 2-8 weeks for electronic filers and 12+ weeks for paper filers. The NOA shows:
Whether CRA accepted your return as filed or made changes
Your refund amount or balance owing
Your RRSP deduction limit for the next year
Your unused RRSP carry-forward amounts
If You Disagree with CRA
Review the NOA carefully for errors
File a T1-ADJ (T1 Adjustment Request) for simple corrections
File a Notice of Objection within 90 days of the NOA date for formal disputes
Contact a tax professional if the dispute involves significant amounts
Record Keeping Requirements
Keep all tax records, receipts, and supporting documents for at least 6 years
Self-employed: Keep business records for 6 years from the end of the tax year they relate to
Store records securely — CRA can request them if your return is reviewed or audited
Consider storing digital copies alongside paper copies
Always verify the most current rules and deadlines at canada.ca/taxes
